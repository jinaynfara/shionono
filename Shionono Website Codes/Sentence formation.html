Grammar

The sentence construction module will be focused on the arrangement of words such as subject, object, and verb, especially the additional words in different kinds of sentences. This will provide you the next step in being able to understand the Korean Hangul.



Writing Sentences in Korean

한국어로 문장 쓰기


The formulas for writing a sentence

문장을 쓰는 공식

Subject + Verb

주어 + 동사


Examples:

캐럴이 와요
[Carol-i wha-yo]

Subject + verb
Carol comes.

Subject + Object + Word

주어 + 목적어 + 단어


Examples:

에릭이 사과를 먹어요
[Eric-i sa-gwa-leul muk-uh-yo]

Subject + object + verb
Eric eats an apple.


Types of Korean Sentences

Interrogative Sentence

  • Interrogative informal polite sentences are made by adding -아/어요? to the word stem.

  • Interrogative sentences are often determined through intonations and ending marks such as question marks.


    • Examples:

    언제 만날까요?
    When shall we meet?

    왜 한국어를 공부해요?
    Why do you study Korean?


    Declarative Sentence

  • These sentences explain or respond to a certain thought or idea.

  • Declarative informal sentences are made by adding -아/어요 to the word stem.


    • Examples:

    저는 학교에가요
    I go to school.

    저는 빵을먹어요
    I eat bread.

    Imperative sentences

  • these sentences are made by adding ~(으)십시오 to the word stem.

  • these are often used for demanding or advising.


    • Examples:

    음식 먹으세요
    Please eat the food.

    밥을 천천히 먹어요
    Eat your food slowly


    Propositive sentences

  • These sentences are used in agreeing to an idea or thought.

  • It is made by adding ~(으)ㅂ시다 to the word stem.


    • Examples:

    여기에서 저녁을 먹읍시다
    Let's have dinner here.

    10시에 만나요
    Let's meet at 10 O'clock.
